Teaching Accessibility in Web Developement

I am taking a class at Udacity on front end web development so that I can learn how to build webpages. The one thing I think is missing in the course is an emphasis on making sure the pages and sites being built are accessible to all users. I am a person who is legally blind and this is a subject that is very important to me.

Teaching future web designers to think about accessibility from the start will create coders who consider how there work will be used by a variety of consumers. I don't believe this will add much to the course and encouraging learners to think about coding in the right way from the start can only be a positive. I hope this is something that the curriculum writers will consider in the future.
